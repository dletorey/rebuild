## School

When I was at school I really didn’t like English, but this was not because of the books we read. One of the books that we studied was [_A Kestrel for a Knave_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Kestrel_for_a_Knave) by [Barry Hines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barry_Hines). 

Once we had finished reading the book we were shown the film [Kes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kes_(film)) based on the book and directed by [Ken Loach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ken_Loach). It remains to this day one of my favourite films and was also [voted number 7 in the greatest British films of the 20th Century](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BFI_Top_100_British_films).

It resonated with me so much because Billy Casper’s experience at school was kind of similar to mine. I was bullied, I was caned, I hated PE (especially the showers) and during my careers interview I was asked what job I wanted to do at the Steelworks.

## T-Shirt

In 1993 Chumbawumba released a single Timebomb which featured [David Bradley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dai_Bradley), who portrayed Billy Casper in the film, flicking a "V" sign (aggressively holding up two fingers) on the cover. 

I bought the Timebomb t-shirt when I saw Chumbawumba at [Heineken free music festival in Leeds Roundhay Park](https://www.setlist.fm/festival/1993/heineken-music-festival-leeds-1993-7bd496a0.html)

## Teaching

From September 1999 to January 2001 I worked as a supply teacher in Sheffield and Barnsley. One of the schools that I worked at was [Edward Sheerien School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Sheerien_School), the school that was used for the filming of Kes. A number of the teachers that I worked with had attended the school and were extras in the film.

## Film with live music score

Today Susan and I went to the [Southbank Centre](https://www.southbankcentre.co.uk/) where as part of their 75th anniversary and [Rough Trade’s](https://www.roughtrade.com/) 50th anniversary they were showing [Kes](https://www.southbankcentre.co.uk/whats-on/kes-live-soundtrack/) with a live orchestra playing the film score. 

Before they showed the film there was a Q&A with [Miranda Sawyer](https://www.southbankcentre.co.uk/bio/miranda-sawyer/) questioning David Bradley, [John Cameron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Cameron_(musician)) (composer of the film score) and [Jarvis Cocker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jarvis_Cocker) (of Pulp and Sheffield fame).

![A screen showing a poster for Kes the film, beneath it is an orchestra](/images/books/kes-orchestra.jpg)

This was the first time I have seen the film on a huge screen and it was as emotional as I expected.
